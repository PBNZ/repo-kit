# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want this project to be a welcoming, respectful, and productive space for everyone — regardless
of background, identity, or experience level.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful, patient, and generous in interpretation.
- Offer and accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Acknowledge mistakes, apologise when appropriate, and move forward.
- Focus on what is best for the project and its users.
- Use clear, professional language in issues, pull requests, commit messages, and discussions.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning comments.
- Discriminatory or exclusionary language or conduct of any kind.
- Unwanted personal attention or contact.
- Publishing another person's private information without their explicit consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or reviews.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — issues, pull requests, commit messages, code
review comments, and any other connected forum — and to public spaces where someone is representing
the project.

## Reporting

Report violations privately to the maintainers using GitHub's **Report abuse** flow on the relevant
user, issue, or comment, or by opening a private security advisory. Reports are handled
confidentially.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate — removing or rejecting contributions, issuing
warnings, or temporarily/permanently banning contributors for inappropriate, threatening, or harmful
behaviour.
